The legal framework, by jurisdiction

Each jurisdiction has its own law. All require WCAG-aligned captions on video.

🇬🇧

United Kingdom

PSBAR 2018 · Equality Act 2010 · UK GDPR
Compliance deadlines: already passed

The Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (PSBAR) require all UK public sector bodies — including universities — to meet WCAG 2.2 Level AA for their digital content. Pre-recorded video with audio must have synchronised closed captions. All compliance deadlines under PSBAR have already passed (new content: 2018; existing content: 2019–2020). Universities also have an anticipatory duty under Section 20 of the Equality Act 2010 to make reasonable adjustments for disabled students and staff — including making video content accessible without requiring individual requests.

Source: legislation.gov.uk (PSBAR 2018); legislation.gov.uk (Equality Act 2010); JISC Accessibility resources (jisc.ac.uk)

🇪🇺

European Union

Web Accessibility Directive 2016/2102 · EN 301 549 · EU GDPR
Compliance deadlines: already passed

EU Directive 2016/2102 (the Web Accessibility Directive) requires all EU public sector bodies to meet the harmonised European standard EN 301 549, which incorporates WCAG 2.1 Level AA. This covers university websites, intranets, extranets, and mobile apps. All compliance deadlines have passed (new sites: 2018; existing sites: 2019; mobile apps: 2021). Member states are required to monitor and report compliance to the European Commission every three years. Universities must also publish an accessibility statement and provide a mechanism for users to report failures.

Source: European Commission digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu; W3C WAI (w3.org)

🇺🇸

United States

ADA Title II · Section 504 · FERPA
Deadline: 26 April 2027 (entities serving 50,000+ population)

The DOJ's 2024 final rule under ADA Title II requires public universities (as state entities) to bring web content and video into conformance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. A DOJ Interim Final Rule published 20 April 2026 extended the original compliance date by one year to 26 April 2027 for entities serving populations of 50,000 or more. Universities receiving federal funding are also covered by Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, which has required effective communication accessibility since 1973. The underlying obligations predate the 2027 deadline.

Source: U.S. Department of Justice, ada.gov; U.S. Department of Education, ed.gov

⚠️

The GDPR / UK GDPR risk cloud captioning creates: Lecture recordings containing identifiable students or staff constitute personal data. Under UK GDPR and EU GDPR Article 28, any third party that processes personal data on your behalf must be designated as a Data Processor in a written Data Processing Agreement (DPA). Sending footage to a consumer cloud captioning service without a compliant DPA means your institution remains fully liable for any breach, misuse, or onward transfer of that data. In the US, FERPA imposes the equivalent obligation under a "school official" designation agreement.

Vosuba eliminates this risk entirely: processing happens locally on your Mac. No footage is uploaded. No DPA is required.

Source: UK Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk); EU GDPR Article 28 (gdpr-info.eu); U.S. Department of Education FERPA guidance (ed.gov)

Common questions from accessibility teams

Does PSBAR apply to all university video content, including internal staff training?

PSBAR applies to content published on public-facing websites and apps. Intranet content accessed only by employees may be exempt — but the Equality Act 2010 still applies and requires reasonable adjustments for disabled staff. The safe and legally defensible position is to caption all institutional video.

Source: JISC Accessibility guidance (jisc.ac.uk)

Does GDPR apply when we use a captioning service for lecture recordings?

Yes, if the recording contains identifiable individuals (students, lecturers). Under UK GDPR / EU GDPR Article 28, you must have a written Data Processing Agreement with any third-party processor before transferring the footage. Your institution remains the Data Controller and is fully liable for the processor's compliance. Vosuba processes all video locally — no footage is ever transferred to us.

Source: ICO (ico.org.uk); GDPR Article 28 text (gdpr-info.eu)

Are automated captions from YouTube or Teams sufficient for compliance?

No. UK guidance from JISC and the ICO, and US guidance from Section508.gov, both note that automatically generated captions typically do not meet the accuracy threshold required for WCAG compliance. A layer of human review or AI-assisted editing is required. Vosuba generates a high-quality AI draft that you edit — significantly faster than correcting raw ASR output.

Source: Section508.gov; JISC (jisc.ac.uk)

Can we claim disproportionate burden to avoid captioning?

Under PSBAR, a "disproportionate burden" exemption is possible but has a high threshold — it requires a documented assessment of cost vs. benefit, formal publication in your accessibility statement, and provision of accessible alternatives wherever possible. Given that Vosuba costs £199/year per workstation, the disproportionate burden argument becomes very difficult to sustain for most departments.

Does Vosuba work on Windows?

Vosuba is currently macOS-only (Apple Silicon M1 or later). It is suited for accessibility teams, media departments, and instructional design units that already operate on Mac. Contact us if Windows support is a requirement — we are evaluating it for a future release.
