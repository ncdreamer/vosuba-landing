Who has a video captioning obligation?

Any organisation that produces video and is covered by one or more of these laws:

🎓 Higher Education

ADA Title II · Section 504 · FERPA

Public universities are state entities covered by ADA Title II. All web-published and LMS-hosted video — lectures, training, departmental content — must meet WCAG 2.1 AA by April 26, 2027. Lecture recordings containing identifiable students are FERPA-protected education records and cannot be uploaded to cloud services without a compliant Data Processing Agreement.

🚆 Public Transit & Rail

ADA Title II · Section 504 · FTA / FRA

Transit authorities and rail operators are public entities subject to ADA Title II and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. The Federal Transit Administration (FTA) and Federal Railroad Administration (FRA) require that communications — including training and safety videos — are accessible to employees and riders with disabilities.

✈️ Aviation & Airlines

ACAA 14 CFR Part 382 · ADA · Section 504

Under the Air Carrier Access Act (14 CFR Part 382), airlines must ensure training materials are accessible to employees with disabilities. Safety and procedural training videos — which often contain sensitive operational footage — must be captioned as a reasonable accommodation, and may not be appropriately uploaded to consumer cloud tools.

🏛️ Federal Agencies

Section 508 · Rehabilitation Act

Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act requires all federal agencies to caption every pre-recorded and live video — internal training, onboarding, and employee communications included. The technical standard is WCAG 2.0 Level AA. Auto-generated captions from YouTube or Teams typically do not meet the required accuracy threshold (source: Section508.gov).

🏥 Healthcare & Research

Section 504 · HIPAA · ADA Title III

Healthcare organisations receiving federal funding (Medicare/Medicaid) must comply with Section 504. Patient education video, staff training footage, and any recording containing identifiable patient information falls under HIPAA. Uploading HIPAA-protected video to a cloud captioning service without a Business Associate Agreement creates direct regulatory exposure.

🏙️ State & Local Government

ADA Title II · Section 504

Every state and local government entity — city councils, county departments, public schools, emergency services — is covered by ADA Title II. Council meeting recordings, emergency briefings, and public information videos posted online must be WCAG 2.1 AA compliant by April 2027.

Frequently asked questions

Does ADA Title II require universities to caption their videos?

Yes. The DOJ's 2024 final rule under ADA Title II requires public universities (as state entities) to bring their web content and video into conformance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. The compliance deadline for entities serving populations of 50,000 or more is April 26, 2027 — extended from the original date by an interim final rule published April 20, 2026. The underlying obligation to provide effective communication to people with disabilities has existed since the ADA was enacted in 1990.

Source: U.S. Department of Justice, ada.gov

Can we upload student lecture recordings to a cloud captioning service?

Not without a formal, compliant Data Processing Agreement. Under FERPA, video recordings that directly relate to an identifiable student — such as a student's individual presentation, or a seminar recording where students participate — are education records. Institutions remain legally responsible for the data even when it is processed by a third-party vendor. Uploading such recordings to a consumer cloud service without a written agreement designating the vendor as a "school official" may constitute a FERPA violation.

Vosuba processes all video locally on your Mac. Nothing is uploaded. No agreement is required.

Source: U.S. Department of Education, ed.gov; Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), 20 U.S.C. § 1232g

Does Section 508 require federal agencies to caption internal training videos?

Yes. Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act applies to all electronic and information technology developed, procured, maintained, or used by federal agencies — including internal training materials, onboarding videos, and employee communications. The standard is WCAG 2.0 Level AA. Auto-generated captions from platforms like YouTube or Microsoft Teams typically do not meet the required accuracy threshold and must be manually reviewed.

Source: Section508.gov; 29 U.S.C. § 794d

We use Windows across our organisation. Does Vosuba work for us?

Vosuba is currently a macOS-only application. It is best suited for teams that already use Mac workstations — common in media, design, and accessibility departments. If your accessibility team operates on Mac but the rest of your organisation uses Windows, Vosuba can still serve as your compliance captioning workstation. We are evaluating Windows support; contact us if this is a requirement.

Does Vosuba replace professional captioning vendors entirely?

For most standard video content — lectures, training, departmental communications — Vosuba's accuracy on clear audio is suitable for WCAG compliance. For content with significant legal, medical, or evidentiary weight, we recommend human review of AI-generated captions, as with any AI transcription tool. Vosuba dramatically reduces the cost of that review by giving you a high-quality first draft to correct, rather than a blank transcript.
