Your compliance obligations at a glance

The key laws, by sector and jurisdiction.

📌

European Accessibility Act (EAA) — in force since 28 June 2025

  • Unlike the earlier Web Accessibility Directive, the EAA covers private sector companies — not just public bodies.
  • Sectors covered: transport (air, bus, rail), banking, audiovisual media, e-commerce, and more.
  • New products/services: must comply now. Existing products/services (on market before June 2025): must comply by 28 June 2030.
  • Micro-enterprises (<10 employees, <€2M turnover) may qualify for an exemption.
Source: EU Directive 2019/882 (eur-lex.europa.eu)

Which industries are affected — and by which laws

All of the sectors below have enforceable video accessibility obligations across UK, EU, or US law.

✈️ Aviation & Airlines

UK: Equality Act 2010  ·  EU: EAA 2019/882  ·  US: ACAA 14 CFR Part 382
  • US: Air Carrier Access Act requires accessible disability training for all US carriers.
  • UK: Equality Act duty to make reasonable adjustments for disabled staff — including captioned training video.
  • EU: EAA covers passenger transport digital services from June 2025.
  • Safety & operational footage often can’t be uploaded to third-party cloud services.

Source: transportation.gov; legislation.gov.uk; EU Directive 2019/882

🚆 Rail & Public Transit

UK: Equality Act 2010  ·  EU: EAA 2019/882  ·  US: ADA Title II · FTA
  • EU: EAA explicitly covers rail, bus, and waterborne transport — websites, apps, and information services from June 2025.
  • UK: Rail operators and Transport for London are subject to Equality Act service provider duties.
  • US: Public transit agencies are governed by ADA Title II and Federal Transit Administration oversight.

Source: EU Directive 2019/882; transit.dot.gov

🏥 Healthcare & Life Sciences

UK: Equality Act · UK GDPR  ·  EU: EU GDPR  ·  US: Section 504 · HIPAA
  • Staff training and patient education video must be captioned for accessibility.
  • UK/EU: Patient footage is special category data under GDPR — cloud upload without a DPA creates serious regulatory exposure.
  • US: Patient-identifiable footage requires a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement before any third-party processing.

Source: ico.org.uk; HHS.gov

📺 Audiovisual Media & Streaming

EU: EAA 2019/882 · AVMS Directive  ·  UK: Ofcom  ·  US: FCC
  • EU EAA: covers streaming platforms, electronic programme guides, and related consumer devices from June 2025.
  • EU AVMS Directive: media service providers must progressively improve accessibility.
  • UK: Ofcom sets captioning quotas for licensed broadcasters.
  • US: Online video providers distributing broadcast content face FCC captioning requirements.

Source: EU Directive 2019/882; Ofcom Broadcasting Code; fcc.gov

🏗️ Government & Public Services

UK: PSBAR 2018  ·  EU: Web Accessibility Directive  ·  US: ADA Title II · Section 508
  • UK: PSBAR 2018 requires WCAG 2.2 AA for all public sector bodies — deadlines have passed.
  • EU: Web Accessibility Directive requires EN 301 549 / WCAG 2.1 AA — deadlines have passed.
  • US: Section 508 requires captions on all federal agency video. ADA Title II deadline for state/local government: 26 April 2027.
  • Government footage often includes sensitive operational briefings that must stay on-premises.

Source: legislation.gov.uk; Section508.gov; ada.gov

🏦 Banking & Financial Services

EU: EAA 2019/882  ·  UK: Equality Act 2010  ·  US: ADA Title III
  • EU EAA: consumer banking services (online banking, websites, apps) must comply from June 2025.
  • UK: Equality Act service provider duties apply to digital content including video.
  • Internal training footage often includes sensitive market data not suitable for cloud processing.

Source: EU Directive 2019/882; legislation.gov.uk

⚠️

Cloud captioning creates a GDPR Article 28 problem for regulated industries.

  • Any video containing identifiable employees, customers, or patients is personal data under UK/EU GDPR.
  • Uploading it to a cloud vendor makes them a Data Processor — requiring a written Data Processing Agreement before processing starts.
  • In regulated industries, a processor breach involving safety incidents or patient interactions can have severe consequences.

Frequently asked questions

Does the European Accessibility Act apply to private companies?

Yes — this is what distinguishes the EAA from the earlier Web Accessibility Directive, which only covered public sector bodies. The EAA (Directive 2019/882) applies to private companies providing covered products and services (transport, banking, e-commerce, audiovisual media, and more) to consumers in the EU. It became enforceable on 28 June 2025. Existing products/services on the market before that date have until 28 June 2030 to comply. Micro-enterprises (<10 employees, <€2M turnover) may be exempt.

Source: EU Directive 2019/882; eur-lex.europa.eu

Does UK law still mirror EU law post-Brexit?

Not automatically, but the UK has its own equivalent laws that impose comparable obligations. PSBAR 2018 mirrors the Web Accessibility Directive for UK public sector bodies. UK GDPR mirrors EU GDPR. The Equality Act 2010 imposes disability accommodation duties. The UK has not yet enacted a direct equivalent of the EAA for private sector digital services, but the Equality Act's "reasonable adjustment" duties apply to service providers broadly. UK organisations selling into the EU are also subject to the EAA for their EU-facing products and services.
